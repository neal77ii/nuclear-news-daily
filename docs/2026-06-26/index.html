Launching into tomorrow: NRIC guides new era of research and deployment

As part of Idaho National Laboratory, the National Reactor Innovation Center has a variety of test beds, programs, and initiatives designed to take nuclear technologies from concept to commercialization. These resources are becoming more important than ever as a new wave of developers are working at pace to bring their first-of-a-kind nuclear technologies to market. To learn how NRIC will leverage these resources across the nuclear sector, NN Associate Editor Lucas Geiger spoke with Brad Tomer, NRIC’s director. Read their full conversation on Nuclear Newswire.

관련 링크:
  • Nuclear Newswire

DOME: Legacy built, future ready

One of the most critical pieces of NRIC’s suite of resources is the DOME—Demonstration of Microreactor Experiments, the newly-opened reactor testbed built in the containment dome that once housed the historic Experimental Breeder Reactor-II. Once slated for demolition, DOME is now set to play a key role in validating the technologies that will be used to advance nuclear energy. Look through a collection of striking photos of DOME’s infrastructure on Nuclear Newswire.

NRC proposes security regulation changes

The NRC proposed its newest set of “sweeping updates” in a series of changes in response to EO 14300. This time around, the agency is aiming to modernize various security regulations. Go deeper on Nuclear Newswire.

NRC news: FY 2026 fees finalized, facility licensing reviews launched

The Nuclear Regulatory Commission's launching of its environmental review of Holtec International's two SMR-300 reactors at the Palisades site in Michigan and its proposed changes to fuel cycle and materials licensing regulations may have grabbed the most attention recently, but the agency has also announced multiple other updates in recent weeks. Go deeper on Nuclear Newswire.

IAEA releases interactive tool on global spent nuclear fuel

With its newly released, online “Spent Fuel Management: The Inventory Status” tool, the IAEA has provided a useful resource for investigating the amount of SNF produced by reactors around the world and how it is currently stored. Go deeper on Nuclear Newswire.

DOE prepares to demolish Oak Ridge’s Isotope Row buildings

Having completed deactivation work, the Oak Ridge Office of Environmental Management is set to begin demolition in July of a cluster of highly contaminated buildings at Oak Ridge National Laboratory in Tennessee. Go deeper on Nuclear Newswire.
