White House to consolidate data and research under AI-driven Genesis Mission

One of the executive orders issued by President Trump in November, “Launching the Genesis Mission,” focuses on a national effort to accelerate the use of AI in scientific research. The Genesis Mission EO preceded the DOE reorganization and tightens links between science and security, in line with the administration’s quest for “global technology dominance in the development of artificial intelligence.” Before AI-powered modeling and automation tools can impact science and research, they need data. In the Genesis Mission EO, Trump directs the DOE to compile data into an AI platform that the DOE says “will be the world’s most complex and powerful scientific instrument ever built.” Go deeper on Nuclear NewsWire.

Education and training to support Canadian nuclear workforce development

Along with several other nations, Canada has committed to net-zero emissions by 2050. Part of this plan is tripling nuclear generating capacity. As of 2025, the country has four operating nuclear generating stations with a total of 17 reactors, 16 of which are in the province of Ontario. The Independent Electricity System Operator has recommended that an additional 17,800 MWe of nuclear power be added to Ontario’s grid. Go deeper on Nuclear NewsWire.

International universities launch nuclear law courses

Six universities in Africa, Asia, Latin America, and the Caribbean now offer postgraduate courses in nuclear law with the support of the International Atomic Energy Agency, expanding legal education in the nuclear community. The courses are supported by the IAEA’s University Partnership Program on Nuclear Law. Go deeper on Nuclear NewsWire.

📅Upcoming nuclear events
