The newest era of workforce development at ANS

While most attendees of this year’s Annual Conference attended talks on a wide range of high-level topics in the nuclear world, 40 people took part in the American Nuclear Society’s second offering of NUC 101. NUC 101 is an entry-level crash course in nuclear science and technology designed to give those in or adjacent to the industry who are not subject matter experts a comprehensive understanding of the science. Course contents: Over five days, students learned about everything from the history of nuclear to different types of reactors to health physics and much more. There were also three guest lecturers—Mark Peters, Kathryn Huff, and Temitope Taiwo—each of whom answered questions from the students and talked about their careers in the industry. Broader scope: ANS is engaged in a variety of activities in the world of certifications and certificates. These plans were discussed in detail at an executive session at this year’s Annual Conference, where course designers recapped the work achieved so far and their upcoming goals. Read more about NUC 101’s progress and ANS’s plans for further bolstering the area of nuclear credentials on Nuclear NewsWire.

Radiant signs contract on microreactors for the military

California-based microreactor developer Radiant Industries has signed what it calls “the first-ever agreement” to deliver a mass-manufactured nuclear microreactor to a U.S. military base. The contract was signed with the Department of Defense’s Defense Innovation Unit and the U.S. Air Force as part of the Advanced Nuclear Power for Installations program. High-burnup fuel rods arrive at PNNL for testing

Eleven high-burnup fuel rods manufactured by Global Nuclear Fuel have been delivered to Pacific Northwest National Laboratory for a battery of destructive tests. PNNL’s evaluation will provide GNF and the DOE with information about the performance of the fuel, which—like other fuels developed through the DOE’s Accident Tolerant Fuel program—was engineered to handle longer operating cycles, improve fuel cycle economics, and support power uprates for existing light water reactors. Oklo and Lightbridge consider co-located fuel fabrication facility

A strategic collaboration has been launched by Lightbridge and Oklo to explore locating Lightbridge’s fuel fabrication facility within Oklo’s planned advanced fuel manufacturing facility. The collaboration aims to “accelerate the commercialization of advanced nuclear fuels through joint fuel fabrication and research and development, including manufacturing fuel using repurposed plutonium from legacy materials.” Go deeper on Nuclear NewsWire.
