DOE fast tracks test reactor projects: What to know

The race to bring test reactors on line by July 4, 2026, got a boost yesterday when the Department of Energy unveiled the names of 10 companies selected for the Nuclear Reactor Pilot Program—a new pathway that allows reactor authorization outside national labs. The goal? To “construct, operate, and achieve criticality of at least three test reactors using the DOE authorization process” by next Independence Day. Missouri gets DOE grant for radioisotopes facility

The Department of Energy’s Office of Science has committed $20 million to the University of Missouri that—with a matching $20 million from the state government—will support construction of a Radioisotope Science Center (RSC) at the university’s Discovery Ridge research park in Columbia, Mo., projected for completion in early 2029.
